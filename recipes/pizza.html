Pizza

Description

Pizza is a popular Italian dish consisting of a flattened, yeast-leavened wheat dough base, traditionally topped with savory tomato sauce, mozzarella cheese, and olive oil, then baked at high temperatures.

Ingredients

The Dough (Base)

  • Flour: All-purpose or bread flour works best.
  • Yeast: Active dry or instant yeast to make it rise.
  • Warm Water: Activates the yeast (aim for 40–43°C).
  • Salt & Sugar: Enhances flavor and helps the yeast react.
  • Olive Oil: Adds richness and helps with texture.

The Sauce

  • Tomato Base: Use crushed tomatoes, passata, or a simple tomato puree.
  • Garlic: Finely minced or sautéed for aroma.
  • Herbs: Dried oregano or Italian seasoning.
  • Seasoning: A pinch of salt, pepper, and sometimes a dash of sugar to balance acidity.

Toppings

  • Cheese: Mozzarella is the classic choice for its meltability.
  • Fresh Herbs: Basil leaves (added after baking for the best flavor).
  • Veggies: Onions, bell peppers, or mushrooms.

Steps

Prepare the Dough

  1. Proof the yeast: Mix warm water (40–43°C), sugar, and yeast in a bowl. Let it sit for about 10 minutes until it becomes frothy and bubbly.
  2. Mix: Add flour, salt, and olive oil to the yeast mixture. Stir until a rough dough forms.
  3. Knead: Move the dough to a floured surface. Knead by hand for 5–10 minutes until it is smooth, elastic, and bounces back when poked.

Let it Rise

  1. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl and cover it with a damp cloth or plastic wrap.
  2. Let it sit in a warm spot for 1 to 2 hours (or until it has doubled in size).

Assemble the Pizza

  1. Preheat: Set your oven to its highest setting (usually 230°C–260°C / 450°F–500°F) at least 30 minutes before baking.
  2. Shape: Punch down the risen dough to release air bubbles. Stretch or roll it into your desired thickness.
  3. Layer: Spread sauce, then add cheese and toppings.

Bake

  1. Place the pizza on a preheated baking sheet or pizza stone.
  2. Bake for 8–15 minutes until the crust is golden and cheese bubbly.
